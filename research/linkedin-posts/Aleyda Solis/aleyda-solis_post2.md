Traditional Search vs AI Search Optimization Shifts across different areas of optimization 👇  

It has become almost an ideological battle to discuss whether AI search optimization is just doing SEO.

The reality, and independently of whatever you want to call AI Search Optimization (GEO, AEO, ... or just SEO for AI platforms), its impact (still a fraction of traditional search) and if it will or not end up "co-existing" with traditional search, is that there are practical implications and shifts in how we optimize we need to take into account if we want to expand our efforts to it as well: 

🕷️ Crawlability: Different bots/user agents for which you might have specific crawlability rules
📖 Indexability: Lack of CSR JS support by AI bots 
⭐️ Content Relevance: Differences in User Search Behavior / Intents to target 
❤️ Popularity: Bigger Role of third-party Citations / Mentions
🎯 Metrics/Goals: To monitor answer visibility / sentiment to traffic / sales (vs monitoring SERP Rankings to Traffic / sales)

The sooner we start expanding what we do to take into account the above (in a reasonable, cost-effective manner), the better for our projects and clients... I don't care how we call it.
