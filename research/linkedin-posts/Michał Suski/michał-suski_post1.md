# Post by Michał Suski — June 2026

Most SEO content is written like AI has the patience of a person doomscrolling LinkedIn.

It doesn’t.

In my conversation with Ivana Flynn, we talked about why some pages get cited by ChatGPT again and again, while others burn money on content that AI doesn’t even want to finish reading.

The simplest tip?

Stop building suspense. Give the answer immediately.

Don’t start with:

“In today’s fast-changing world…”
“In the age of digital transformation…”
“It depends on many factors…”

Just give the answer in the first sentence.

AI runs on token economy.

If it needs to burn 700 tokens to find the answer on your page, and your competitor gives it in 300 — guess who is easier to cite.

This is old-school journalism: BLUF — Bottom Line Up Front.

First: the answer.
Then: context.
Then: nuance.

And it’s funny, because for years SEO inflated content like a balloon:

more words,
more sections,
more “things you should know before we answer the damn question.”

Now the winner is the one who can say the thing clearly, fast, and without all the fluff.

In the podcast, we also talked about what not to do in AI Search — because there are already plenty of vendors selling magical dashboards that look smart, but measure the wrong thing.
